LoRA: Low-Rank Adaptation of Large Language Models

Hu et al., Microsoft · ICLR 2022 · arXiv 2106.09685

Fine-tuning GPT-3 meant saving a 350GB checkpoint per task. LoRA matches it with a 35MB file — 10,000× fewer trainable parameters, 3× less GPU memory, zero added latency.

the aha · figure 1, redrawn

Napkin math you can verify

Fine-tuning's weight changes turn out to have low intrinsic rank — so don't update the giant matrix at all. Freeze it, learn the update as B·A with rank r as small as 1–8, and merge at inference for zero added latency. On GPT-3 175B: 10,000× fewer trainable parameters, 3× less GPU memory, a per-task checkpoint shrinking from ~350GB to ~35MB — with on-par or better quality than full fine-tuning across the tasks studied. Instead of re-saving a 350GB spreadsheet to share your edits, you ship a compact formula file.

what it changed

Fine-tuning became a product feature

Any "train on your data" feature you use today is almost certainly LoRA-family (or its quantized cousin QLoRA). The architecture unlock is worth designing around: one frozen base model + N per-customer 35MB adapters, hot-swapped per request — multi-tenant customization without N model copies. Rule of thumb: LoRA for style, format, and task behavior; RAG for knowledge.

honest limits

It doesn't teach the model facts

The misconception: "fine-tuning injects new knowledge." LoRA's capacity is tiny by construction — that's the point. It excels at style, tone, format, and task behavior; for fresh or changing facts, retrieval is usually the right tool.

And "cheap" does not mean "worse": the paper reports on-par or better quality than full fine-tuning on RoBERTa, DeBERTa, GPT-2, and GPT-3 — but only across the tasks studied. Push an adapter far outside the base model's competence and the low-rank bottleneck is exactly that: a bottleneck.

Build it yourself

build it: $0 · one evening

  1. read

    Read the paper — Figure 1 (big frozen W, skinny trainable bypass B·A) plus napkin arithmetic is the whole method: a d×k update costs d·k numbers; the rank-r version costs d·r + r·k.

    1–2 hrs · $0

  2. run

    Fine-tune a 1–3B model on your own MacBook with mlx_lm.lora and 200–500 examples — re-use the Taylor Swift lyrics from the author's taylor-lstm for the perfect callback: same data, LSTM in 2023, LoRA'd LLM now. Print trainable vs total params and see <1%.
