Attention Is All You Need

Vaswani et al., Google Brain · NeurIPS 2017 · arXiv 1706.03762

Eight GPUs, three and a half days, one equation — and the machine-translation record fell to an architecture that deleted the thing everyone thought language required: reading in order.

the aha · equation 1, redrawn

One equation, read as a lookup

Read it as a fuzzy database query. Each token issues a query (Q — what it's looking for), every token in the window publishes a key (K — what it advertises), and softmax turns the query–key match scores into weights; the output is a weighted blend of the values (V — what each token carries). The radical part is what's missing: no recurrence, so every token runs this lookup at once, in parallel, across the whole sequence. That parallelism is what made the numbers land: 28.4 BLEU on WMT-14 English→German — past every prior model, ensembles included — after 3.5 days on 8 GPUs. Better and cheaper is why everyone switched.

The engineer's view

Flip to the grid: every cell is one Q·K comparison — a token against a token. It's triangular because a token can only attend backwards. And it is exactly this N×N shape that makes long context expensive: double the tokens, quadruple the cells. This grid is your latency and your bill.

step 03 / 03

Where it breaks

Now the Winograd sentence: "…because it was too big" — does "it" mean the trophy or the suitcase? A human knows instantly; GPT-2 small's best head can't commit, spreading its attention instead of linking "it" to "trophy." Resolving that needs world knowledge this small model doesn't have. Bigger models push the wall out — none of them remove it.

what it changed

This equation is your API bill

QKT compares every token against every other, so attention cost grows roughly with the square of the context — that's why long prompts are expensive, why KV and prompt caching exist as line items, and why retrieval (RAG) usually beats stuffing everything into the window. A "context window" is literally the set of tokens participating in this lookup, and each new token attends over all the previous ones — which demystifies both mid-context forgetting and why latency climbs as a conversation grows. When you budget an AI feature today, you are budgeting this one equation.

honest limits

The invention was the deletion

The misconception: "this paper invented attention." Attention dates to 2014 — Bahdanau's translation models already used it, bolted onto RNNs that still read one word at a time. The radical move here was subtraction: delete the recurrence and keep only attention, which is what unlocked training over whole sequences in parallel.

And it's a machine-translation paper. The authors were chasing a BLEU score on WMT-14; nothing in the text predicts chatbots. The architecture's generality was discovered later — by the papers that follow on this timeline, one scale-up at a time.
