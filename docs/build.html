the doors

Where a first contribution lands

Open-source AI is not a spectator sport, and these repos merge outsiders' work every week. As of July 2026, each of these had open good-first-issue labels or an explicit contributor pipeline:

  • lm-evaluation-harness — the most accessible real contribution for this site's audience: adding or fixing a benchmark task is mostly YAML plus a little Python, and eval literacy is directly job-relevant.
  • vLLM — the inference engine behind most LLM serving; docs, model-support PRs, perf fixes. Best fit for backend engineers.
  • llama.cpp — local inference in C/C++; quantization formats and platform fixes (great for Mac users).
  • diffusers — pipelines, docs, community examples.
  • open-r1 — the community R1 reproduction; training recipes, eval scripts, dataset work.
  • Axolotl — fine-tuning configs, docs, integration fixes.

the rooms

Where the work happens in public

  • EleutherAI — open research done in public; join the Discord, read #research, pick a project. Their SOAR program runs mentored cohorts built explicitly for people with no prior research experience — watch that page for the next one.
  • GPU MODE — weekly GPU-programming lectures and a kernel leaderboard NVIDIA engineers compete on; the door into systems work.
  • Hugging Face — the town square: free LLM, diffusion, and smol courses, each with an active channel.
  • Cohere Labs Open Science — built to "create more points of entry into ML research."
  • The mechanistic-interpretability on-ramp — the free ARENA curriculum; unusually welcoming to career-switchers with only toy-model experience.
  • r/LocalLLaMA — where fine-tuning and quantization practice actually circulates.

the jobs

What labs actually hire for

The research door is narrow: as of March 2026, research roles were 12% of Anthropic's open positions and 7% of OpenAI's (Epoch AI's analysis of lab job postings). The doors that doubled in a year are the ones this site's audience already stands in front of: customer-adoption engineering — Forward Deployed Engineer, Solutions Architect, Applied AI Engineer — roles that want product engineering plus model fluency: prompting, evals, fine-tuning, serving. Not a PhD.

The scarce skill isn't inventing the next transformer — it's having actually trained, aligned, evaluated, and served a model at any scale. Which is precisely what the ladder above produces.
