colophon · how this was made

Nothing here is copied

Every figure on this site is redrawn from data; every claim traces to a paper or a dated source; every borrowed pixel is licensed and credited. This page is the receipts.

figures

Redrawn-figure policy

No figure is reproduced from a paper's PDF. Diagrams (the residual block, the LoRA decomposition, the CLIP grid, pipeline diagrams) are redrawn as original SVG from the papers' published descriptions and numbers. Data charts are rebuilt from values stated in the papers' text and tables; where a value exists only inside a printed figure, points are traced and the chart's data file carries a "traced, approximate" provenance flag, surfaced in its caption. Data files live in docs/data/ with the baker script named in each file.

numbers

Prices, dates, and decay

Dollar figures for training compute use mid-2026 GPU rental prices ($2.50/H100-hour central estimate, 40% utilization) — stamped "prices as of 2026-07" wherever they appear and stored in one dated file, prices.json. GPU prices fell roughly 70% between late 2024 and 2026; historical models cost far more on the hardware of their day. Community and job-market facts on the 2026: you page are stamped "checked July 2026". If you're reading this much later: the shapes hold, the numbers decay.

Timeline positions use each paper's arXiv v1 date; citation chips cite the publication venue. That's why ResNet sits at 2015 but cites CVPR 2016, Latent Diffusion sits at Dec 2021 but cites CVPR 2022, and MoE/LoRA/CoT sit months before their venues. The discrepancy is intentional: the timeline records when the idea arrived.

Embeds, licenses, credits

  • Transformer Explainer and Diffusion Explainer (Georgia Tech Polo Club) are embedded under their MIT licenses, click-to-activate, with attribution beside each embed.
  • LLM Visualization (Brendan Bycroft) is linked out only — it carries no open license, so it is never framed, forked, or rehosted here.

  • The Constitutional AI principles quoted on the Constitutional AI page are verbatim from the paper's appendix (Bai et al., arXiv 2212.08073, App. C.1 & C.2).
  • Fonts: Literata, Inter, and JetBrains Mono via Google Fonts (OFL). The cream paper-chips deliberately use your system's own serif — when the paper speaks, it speaks in print.

design

The system, briefly

Dark graphite ground; five category accents (position on the timeline is chronology's, color is the category's); one continuous SVG loss curve as the spine, drawn from measured DOM positions as you scroll. Cream "paper-chips" mark every place the paper itself speaks. Static HTML with no build step — the full design spec and source are on GitHub. This is the third sibling of taylor-transformer (the machine learns to write) and dogs-vs-cats (the machine learns to see).

curation

Why fourteen

The list was curated paper-by-paper with one lens: not "what shaped AI history," but what changes how an engineer, PM, or founder builds, prioritizes, or places bets today. The full keep/drop record — including what was cut and why (word2vec, GANs, BERT, AlphaGo, Chinchilla, FlashAttention…) — is in PAPERS.md. Disagree with a cut? The repo takes issues.
