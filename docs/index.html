Act I · 2012–2015

The bet on scale

A generic network fed with GPUs and data crushes a decade of hand-engineered vision, and then depth itself gets fixed with two characters of algebra. The bet that opens the curve: general methods that scale beat clever methods that don't.

2012

AlexNet

Two gaming GPUs beat a decade of hand-built vision — by ten points, in a contest where progress was measured in one.

General methods that scale with compute and data beat expert hand-engineering — the bet every paper after this one repeats.

2015

ResNet

A 56-layer network was losing to its own 20-layer version — until two characters of algebra, '+ x', made 152 layers trainable and swept ImageNet.

That '+ x' skip connection now sits inside every Transformer block — the residual stream your interpretability tools talk about is literally this path.

2016: attention already exists — invented in 2014 as a patch bolted onto recurrent networks. Nobody has yet tried keeping the patch and throwing away the machine.

Act II · 2017

The architecture

One equation replaces reading-in-order with looking-everywhere-at-once, and training goes parallel across the whole sequence. Parallel is what makes everything after this point on the curve physically affordable.

2017

Transformer

Eight GPUs, three and a half days, one equation — and the machine-translation record fell to an architecture that deleted the thing everyone thought language required: reading in order.

Attention compares every token against every other, so cost grows with the square of context — this one equation is your API bill, your latency, and the reason prompt caching exists.

Act III · 2019–2021

The scaling era

Next-token prediction turns out to contain every task, loss falls as a straight line you can budget against, and parameter count decouples from cost per token. Capability becomes something you purchase.

2019

GPT-2

Trained on one dumb objective — predict the next word across 40GB of internet — it started translating, summarizing, and answering questions anyway. Nobody trained it to do any of that.

The default question flips from 'how do I train a model for task X' to 'how do I elicit task X from a model that already learned it.'

2020

Scaling Laws

Plot loss against compute on log axes and the points fall on a dead-straight line for eight orders of magnitude — capability suddenly had a price list you could read with a ruler.

'Wait for the next model' becomes a strategy with a price and a date attached — never build the thing one more order of magnitude erases.

2020

GPT-3

At 175 billion parameters, you can teach it a new task by typing three examples into the prompt — no training run, no weights touched, nothing remembered when you leave.

Few-shot examples are still the cheapest quality lever you own, and in-context learning is the mechanism that makes RAG work at all.

6 min build it: $5 · one evening

✓

2021

Mixture-of-Experts

Google scaled a model toward a trillion parameters while compute per token stayed flat — a learned router sends each token to exactly one expert, and pretraining ran up to 7× faster on the same budget.

Parameter count and inference cost are separate axes now — compare models by active parameters, not headline size, and you'll understand why API prices keep falling as capability rises.

6 min build it: $0–10 · a weekend

Act IV · 2021–2022

Four threads at once

Scale stops being the whole story. Four threads pull at once — seeing (CLIP), generating (Stable Diffusion), customizing (LoRA), and aligning (Chain-of-Thought, RLHF, Constitutional AI) — eighteen months that turn raw models into products.

2021

CLIP

Type 'a photo of a dog' and classification becomes retrieval: trained on 400 million web captions, it matched a supervised model on ImageNet while using zero of its 1.28 million hand-labeled examples.

'Embed both sides into one space, then nearest-neighbor' is the workhorse under every vector DB, semantic search, and RAG retriever you ship.

4 min build it: $0 · one evening

✓

2021

LoRA

Fine-tuning GPT-3 meant saving a 350GB checkpoint per task. LoRA matches it with a 35MB file — 10,000× fewer trainable parameters, 3× less GPU memory, zero added latency.

Fine-tuning becomes a product feature instead of a capital expense — one frozen base model, a swappable 35MB adapter per customer.

5 min build it: $0 · one evening

✓

2021

Stable Diffusion

A 512×512 image is 786,432 numbers; this model paints in a compressed space of 16,384 — and that ~48× discount on every one of ~50 denoising steps is what put image generation on gaming GPUs.

The biggest cost wins come from changing the space you compute in — and shipping open weights against a better-funded closed rival turned its users into its R&D department.

8 min build it: $0 · one evening

2022

Chain-of-Thought

Same model, same weights: show it worked examples with the steps written out and its math-word-problem score triples — 17.9% to 56.9%, from a prompt change you could type yourself.

Answer quality becomes a runtime dial — more reasoning tokens, better answers, bigger bill; that's the extended-thinking toggle you flip today.

7 min build it: $5 · one evening

✓

2022

InstructGPT / RLHF

Human raters preferred a 1.3-billion-parameter model over the 175-billion-parameter GPT-3 — a hundred times smaller, and better, because three training steps taught it to actually try to help.

The quality users feel is post-training, not scale — preference-tuning on your task can beat upgrading to a bigger model, and vendors' different 'personalities' are the reward model showing through.

7 min build it: $0 · one evening

✓

2022

Constitutional AI

Anthropic swapped tens of thousands of human harmlessness labels for 16 plain-English principles the model applies to itself — and got a model that was less harmful without refusing more.

Supervising a model with a model plus written rules is now the standard pattern — LLM-as-judge, self-critique loops, and policy-in-the-prompt all descend from this paper.

7 min build it: $1 · one evening

✓

2023–24: the frontier went closed. GPT-4 arrived as a technical report that named no parameter count, and the best ideas became trade secrets — and then, abruptly, they didn't.

Act V · 2025

The reasoning era

Pure reinforcement learning against an automatic answer-checker teaches a model to reason, and the weights ship openly, matching the best closed lab. The curve keeps descending — the open question is who draws the next segment.

2025

DeepSeek-R1

Reward exactly one thing — 'the final answer is correct' — and competition-math scores climb from 15.6% to 71.0% with zero human reasoning examples. The polished model matched OpenAI's o1 and shipped its weights.

Post-training is where differentiation lives now — if your domain has an automatic success check, it's a candidate for RL, and any closed capability gets replicated openly within months.
